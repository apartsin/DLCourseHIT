Week 12   Part III · Architectures & Representation Learning

Representation Learning

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (3 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Train an autoencoder and a contrastive embedding.
  • Probe and interpret a learned latent space.
  • Reason about what makes a representation useful.

🎓Lecture · 3 hours

0:00–0:1010 minRecap & objectivesConnect to the previous week; state this week's objectives.
0:10–0:2515 minMotivationGood representations make downstream tasks easy; learn them with or without labels.
0:25–1:1045 minAutoencodersEncoder, bottleneck, and decoder.; Undercomplete versus overcomplete.; What the latent space captures.
1:10–1:2010 minBreak
1:20–2:0545 minContrastive and self-supervised learningAugmentation defines what counts as similar.; The contrastive loss.; The SimCLR idea.
2:05–2:3530 minLive demoTrain an autoencoder, interpolate the latent space, and sketch a contrastive setup.
2:35–2:5015 minWrap-up & lab previewRecap the takeaways and brief this week's lab.
2:50–3:0010 minBuffer & questions
Key takeaways.
  • A bottleneck forces useful compression.
  • Augmentation choices define similarity.
  • Learned representations transfer to new tasks.

💻Practice · 2 hours

The practice class follows the lab's Build / Predict & probe / Explain & defend model.

0:00–0:1010 minSetup & briefConfirm environments run; restate the lab goal and deliverables.
0:10–0:2515 minPredictStudents write predictions before coding. Predict the latent-space structure you expect (clusters by class, smooth interpolation).
0:25–1:1045 minBuild (AI assistant welcome)Train an autoencoder and build a simple contrastive embedding.
1:10–1:155 minBreak
1:15–1:4025 minProbeRun controlled experiments and compare results against the predictions.
1:40–1:5515 minExplain & defendProbe the latent space (interpolate, cluster, nearest neighbors), interpret what it captures, and critique an AI-suggested but flawed contrastive loss.
1:55–2:005 minWrap & deliverable checkAn autoencoder and embedding notebook with latent-space visualizations.; An interpretation and the loss critique.
Common pitfalls to pre-empt.
  • A too-large bottleneck just copies the input.
  • For contrastive learning, the augmentation choice defines what counts as similar.
