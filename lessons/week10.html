Week 10   Part III · Architectures & Representation Learning

Recurrent Networks (RNNs)

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (3 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Build a plain RNN for sequence data.
  • Understand recurrence and backpropagation through time.
  • Observe the vanishing-gradient problem directly.

🎓Lecture · 3 hours

0:00–0:1010 minRecap & objectivesConnect to the previous week; state this week's objectives.
0:10–0:2515 minMotivationSequences need memory; how recurrence shares parameters across time, and why it struggles.
0:25–1:1045 minRecurrence and BPTTThe RNN cell and the hidden state.; Unrolling a network across time steps.; Backpropagation through time.
1:10–1:2010 minBreak
1:20–2:0545 minVanishing and exploding gradientsWhy long-range dependencies are hard.; Gradient magnitude across time steps.; Gradient clipping.
2:05–2:3530 minLive demoTrain an RNN and plot the gradient norm versus time step to show vanishing.
2:35–2:5015 minWrap-up & lab previewRecap the takeaways and brief this week's lab.
2:50–3:0010 minBuffer & questions
Key takeaways.
  • RNNs share weights across time steps.
  • Long-range gradients vanish or explode.
  • Clip gradients to stabilize training.

💻Practice · 2 hours

The practice class follows the lab's Build / Predict & probe / Explain & defend model.

0:00–0:1010 minSetup & briefConfirm environments run; restate the lab goal and deliverables.
0:10–0:2515 minPredictStudents write predictions before coding. Predict how the gradient magnitude changes across time steps for long sequences.
0:25–1:1045 minBuild (Claude welcome)Build a plain RNN on a character-level or short time-series task.
1:10–1:155 minBreak
1:15–1:4025 minProbeRun controlled experiments and compare results against the predictions.
1:40–1:5515 minExplain & defendMeasure gradient norms across time steps, demonstrate vanishing gradients on long sequences, and explain why long-range dependencies are hard for a plain RNN.
1:55–2:005 minWrap & deliverable checkAn RNN notebook.; A gradient-norm-versus-time-step plot with an explanation.
Common pitfalls to pre-empt.
  • Clip gradients to avoid explosion; start with short sequences.
  • Log the gradient norm at the earliest time steps to see vanishing.
