Week 3   Part I · Foundations

MLPs & Backpropagation

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (3 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Build and train a multilayer perceptron.
  • Understand the forward pass and backpropagation.
  • Use autograd correctly and verify a gradient by hand.

🎓Lecture · 3 hours

0:00–0:1010 minRecap & objectivesConnect to the previous week; state this week's objectives.
0:10–0:2515 minMotivationFrom a single linear layer to a universal function approximator, and how learning actually happens.
0:25–1:1045 minThe multilayer perceptronLinear layers composed with nonlinear activations.; Why the nonlinearity is what makes depth useful.; The forward pass and nn.Module.
1:10–1:2010 minBreak
1:20–2:0545 minBackpropagation and autogradThe chain rule on a computational graph.; Each gradient tells its weight how to change.; autograd, .backward(), zero_grad, and gradient checking.
2:05–2:3530 minLive demoBuild an MLP, inspect .grad, and compare a hand-derived gradient to autograd.
2:35–2:5015 minWrap-up & lab previewRecap the takeaways and brief this week's lab.
2:50–3:0010 minBuffer & questions
Key takeaways.
  • Nonlinearity is what makes depth worthwhile.
  • Backpropagation is the chain rule on a graph.
  • Autograd automates gradients, but you must zero them each step.

💻Practice · 2 hours

The practice class follows the lab's Build / Predict & probe / Explain & defend model.

0:00–0:1010 minSetup & briefConfirm environments run; restate the lab goal and deliverables.
0:10–0:2515 minPredictStudents write predictions before coding. Predict the sign and rough magnitude of one weight's gradient after a single step on a toy example.; Predict how training changes if you remove the nonlinearity.
0:25–1:1045 minBuild (Claude welcome)Implement an MLP (nn.Module) and train it on a small classification task with autograd.; Implement the backward pass for one linear layer by hand (manual tensor ops).
1:10–1:155 minBreak
1:15–1:4025 minProbeRun controlled experiments and compare results against the predictions.
1:40–1:5515 minExplain & defendVerify your hand-computed gradient matches autograd within tolerance and explain any difference.; Explain in words what each gradient tells its weight to do.
1:55–2:005 minWrap & deliverable checkAn MLP notebook with training results.; The hand-derived gradient plus the autograd check.
Common pitfalls to pre-empt.
  • Zero the gradients each step; compare against torch.autograd.gradcheck.
  • Without a nonlinearity an MLP collapses to a linear model.
