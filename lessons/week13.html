Week 13   Part IV · Integration

Integration & Transfer Learning

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (3 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Fine-tune a pretrained model end-to-end.
  • Run inference and assemble a full workflow.
  • Reason about when transfer learning helps.

🎓Lecture · 3 hours

0:00–0:1010 minRecap & objectivesConnect to the previous week; state this week's objectives.
0:10–0:2515 minMotivationYou rarely train from scratch; standing on pretrained models is the bridge to the advanced courses.
0:25–1:1045 minTransfer learningFixed feature extraction versus fine-tuning.; When each strategy works (data size and similarity).; Layer-wise learning rates.
1:10–1:2010 minBreak
1:20–2:0545 minThe end-to-end workflowData, model, train, evaluate, infer.; Matching preprocessing to the pretrained model.; Inference and deployment basics.
2:05–2:3530 minLive demoFine-tune a pretrained ResNet and compare from-scratch, frozen-features, and fine-tuning.
2:35–2:5015 minWrap-up & lab previewRecap the takeaways and brief this week's lab.
2:50–3:0010 minBuffer & questions
Key takeaways.
  • Transfer learning beats training from scratch on small data.
  • Freeze first, then unfreeze gradually.
  • Match the pretrained model's input preprocessing.

💻Practice · 2 hours

The practice class follows the lab's Build / Predict & probe / Explain & defend model.

0:00–0:1010 minSetup & briefConfirm environments run; restate the lab goal and deliverables.
0:10–0:2515 minPredictStudents write predictions before coding. Predict the ranking of from-scratch, fine-tuning, and frozen-features under a fixed budget.
0:25–1:1045 minBuild (AI assistant welcome)Fine-tune a pretrained model end-to-end on a new task and run inference.
1:10–1:155 minBreak
1:15–1:4025 minProbeRun controlled experiments and compare results against the predictions.
1:40–1:5515 minExplain & defendCompare the three regimes, explain when transfer learning helps and why, and reflect on the full workflow.
1:55–2:005 minWrap & deliverable checkA fine-tuning notebook with a three-regime comparison.; A reflection that doubles as final-project preparation. The final project is due this week.
Common pitfalls to pre-empt.
  • Use a smaller learning rate for pretrained layers; unfreeze gradually.
  • Match input preprocessing to the pretrained model.
