Week 8   Part III · Architectures & Representation Learning

Convolutional Networks I

Instructor lesson plan: lecture (3 h) and practice (2 h).

Learning objectives

  • Build and train a CNN image classifier.
  • Understand convolution, pooling, and feature maps.
  • Compute how shapes and parameters change layer by layer.

🎓Lecture · 3 hours

0:00–0:1010 minRecap & objectivesConnect to the previous week; state this week's objectives.
0:10–0:2515 minMotivationWhy fully-connected nets waste parameters on images, and how convolution exploits structure.
0:25–1:1045 minConvolution and poolingFilters, feature maps, stride, and padding.; Parameter sharing and locality.; Pooling and downsampling.
1:10–1:2010 minBreak
1:20–2:0545 minBuilding a CNN classifierStacking convolution and pooling layers.; Spatial-size and parameter arithmetic.; From feature maps to a classifier head.
2:05–2:3530 minLive demoBuild a small CNN, compute output shapes by hand, and train on FashionMNIST.
2:35–2:5015 minWrap-up & lab previewRecap the takeaways and brief this week's lab.
2:50–3:0010 minBuffer & questions
Key takeaways.
  • Convolution means local connectivity with shared weights.
  • Output size = floor((in + 2p - k) / s) + 1.
  • Feature maps detect patterns at increasing abstraction.

💻Practice · 2 hours

The practice class follows the lab's Build / Predict & probe / Explain & defend model.

0:00–0:1010 minSetup & briefConfirm environments run; restate the lab goal and deliverables.
0:10–0:2515 minPredictStudents write predictions before coding. Compute by hand the output spatial size and parameter count of each conv and pool layer, and predict them before checking.
0:25–1:1045 minBuild (Claude welcome)Build and train a CNN image classifier (for example on MNIST, FashionMNIST, or a CIFAR-10 subset).
1:10–1:155 minBreak
1:15–1:4025 minProbeRun controlled experiments and compare results against the predictions.
1:40–1:5515 minExplain & defendVerify against the model summary, explain any mismatch, and explain what a feature map represents.
1:55–2:005 minWrap & deliverable checkA CNN notebook with accuracy.; The hand-computed shape-and-parameter table versus the model summary.
Common pitfalls to pre-empt.
  • Output size = floor((in + 2p - k) / s) + 1.
  • Conv parameters = (k * k * Cin + 1) * Cout; verify with a summary tool.
